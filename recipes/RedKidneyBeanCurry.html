Red Kidney Bean Curry

Ingredients


250g dried red kidney beans
1 tbsp butter
2 tbsps oil
1 onion, chopped
cinnamon
2 dried bay leaves
3 garlic cloves
2 tsps grated ginger
1/2 tsp tumeric
1 tsp ground coriander
2 tsps ground cumin
1 tsp garam masala
2 dried red chillies
4 tbsp tomato purée
salt and pepper
yoghurt to drizzle (optional)

Put the red kidney beans in a large saucepan, cover with cold water and let soak overnight.
Drain the soaked beans and return to the saucepan with double the amount of water. Bring to the boil, then keep boiling for 15 minutes.

Reduce the heat to medium/low and simmer gently for 1 hour, or until the beans are tender. Drain, reserving the cooking liquid.
Heat the butter and oil in a large, heavy-based saucepan and add the onion, cinnamon, bay leaves, garlic, and ginger, and stri-fry for 4-5 minutes.
Stir in the turmeric, ground coriander, garam masala, and chillies.

Add the beans, tomato purée and enough of the reserved cooking liquid to make a thick sauce. Bring to the boil and cook for 4-5 minutes, stirring often.
Serve, with yoghurt if desired.
