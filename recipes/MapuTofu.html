Mapu Tofu

Ingredients


8 ounces shiitake mushrooms
2 cups water
15 ounce block of soft tofu (not silken)
Salt
3 tbsp olive oil
3 small dried hot red peppers
1 tbsp femented black beans
1 tbsp fermented spicy broad bean paste (doubanjiang)
2 tsp minced garlice
1 tbsp grated ginger
2 tsp soy sauce
1 tsp taoasted sesame oil
1 tsp finel ground Sichuan pepper
1 tbsp (corn/potato) starch, dissolved in 3 tbsp cold water
1/2 cup slivered scallions (spring onions)

Remove stems from mushrooms.
Make a light mushroom broth by simmering stems in 2 cups water for 15 minutes, then strain and reserve broth (discard stems).
Dice mushroom caps and set aside.

Cut tofu into 1-inch cubes.
Cover with boiling salted water, let steep for 15 minutes, then drain.

Put oil in a wok or wide skillet over medium heat.
Add red peppers, balck beans, and bea paste and cook, stirring until fragrant, about 1 minute.
Add garlic and ginger and let sizzle, then add mushrooms, soy sauce, sasame oil and Sichuan pepper.
Add 1.5 cups mushroom broth and cook mixture gently for 2 minutes.

Carefully add tofu cubes.
Shake pan to distribute sauce, using a wooden spoon to help.
Try to avoid smashing tofu.
Drizzle in cornstarch mixture, gently swirling pan to incorporate (sauce will thicken) and simmer tofu in sauce for 2 minutes more.
Thin with a little mushroom broth if necessary.
Transfer to a low bowl or platter.
Sprinkle with scallions.
